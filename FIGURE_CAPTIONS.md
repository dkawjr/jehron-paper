# Figure captions

Captions for every figure in this repository. Counts are from the 47 positive
GI-PCR detections (in 41 patients); risk-factor estimates are from the cohort of
90 patients.

**Figure 1. Pathogens detected on positive GI-PCR tests.**
Distribution of the 47 pathogen detections among the 41 patients with a positive
GI-PCR. *Escherichia coli* was most common (17; 36%), followed by norovirus
(10; 21%) and *Cryptosporidium* (6; 13%). Bars are coloured by pathogen type.

**Figure 2. Detections over time, by pathogen type.**
Positive detections binned by day after HSCT (100–400, 400–700, 700–1000,
>1000). The total number of detections falls over time (n = 26, 11, 6 and 4 per
interval). Bacteria are present in every interval; viruses and parasites are
concentrated in the first interval.

**Figure 3. Detections over time, by individual pathogen.**
Same intervals as Figure 2, split by individual organism. Segments are shaded
within pathogen type.

**Figure 4. Relative contribution of each pathogen type over time.**
Composition of detections within each interval (100% stacked). The number of
detections per interval (n) is printed above each bar; the later intervals rest
on very few detections.

**Figure 5. Time to first positive GI-PCR by GVHD status (Kaplan–Meier).**
*Not yet generated — this needs the per-patient survival dataset (time, event,
GVHD status). The script is ready and will produce the figure once that file is
supplied.*

**Figure 6. Adjusted hazard ratios for a positive GI-PCR >100 days after HSCT.**
Multivariable Cox model. Female sex (HR 0.48, 95% CI 0.26–0.90) and absence of
early GVHD (HR 2.59, 1.12–5.96) were independently associated with a positive
result. Reference levels are marked; the dashed line is HR = 1.

**Figure 7. Unadjusted odds ratios for a positive GI-PCR.**
Reconstructed from the 2×2 counts in Table 1. Absence of early GVHD (OR 3.69,
1.31–10.45) and not being hospitalised at the time of testing (hospitalised vs
not, OR 0.38, 0.16–0.92) were associated with a positive result; the remaining
factors were not. Intervals crossing OR = 1 are shown in grey.

**Figure 8. Detection counts by pathogen and time interval (heatmap).**
Rows are coloured by pathogen type; both the shading and the printed number
encode the count in each cell.

**Figure 9. Cumulative detections over time, by pathogen type.**
Cumulative number of detections against exact day after HSCT (bacteria n = 24,
virus n = 14, parasite n = 8; the single "other" detection is not plotted, so the
curves cover 46 of 47 detections). The rug marks individual detections. This is a
cumulative count, not an incidence or hazard estimate.

**Figure 10. Every detection by exact day after HSCT.**
All 47 detections plotted individually, one row per pathogen, coloured by type.

**Figure 11. Study flow.**
373 records were reviewed; 90 met inclusion criteria; 41 (46%) had a positive
GI-PCR (47 detections) and 49 (54%) were negative.

**Figure 12. Microbiologic spectrum and its timing (composite).**
(a) Overall pathogen frequency; (b) every detection by exact day; (c) cumulative
share of each pathogen type's detections over time.

**Figure 13. Risk factors for a positive GI-PCR (composite).**
(a) Unadjusted odds ratios for all candidate factors; (b) adjusted hazard ratios
from the Cox model. Panel a reports odds ratios and panel b hazard ratios.

**Figure 14. Positivity and composition with 95% confidence intervals.**
Wilson 95% CIs for the headline proportions. 41/90 patients were positive (46%,
36–56%); among detections, bacteria accounted for 51% (37–65%), viruses 30%
(19–44%) and parasites 17% (9–30%).
